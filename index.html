NeuroMarketing

Neuromarketing is a field that studies how consumers respond to marketing stimuli by measuring brain activity. It uses techniques like eye tracking, electroencephalograms (EEGs). Goal: Understand consumers' reactions to marketing, Improve marketing campaigns and strategies, Reduce product and campaign failures. Techniques: Eye tracking: Uses cameras or glasses to monitor eye position and identify what attracts consumers' attention EEGs: Uses sensors on the scalp to track brain activity Benefits: Provides insights into why consumers don't always act on their intentions Helps companies create more effective ads and branding Helps companies understand how to change marketing to appeal to consumers' emotions Applications Comparing advertising campaigns before releasing them to the public, Improving advertisements and branding.
